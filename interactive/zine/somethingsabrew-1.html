Some Things Abrewing

Today at Christie's New York, the first AI-generated painting sold for $432,500 - it was expected to sell for $10,000. A type of AI algorithm known as Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN) and trained by a group called Obvious art generated the work.

The painting named “Edmond de Belamy, from La Famille de Belamy” fetched a higher price than the Andy Warhol sold a week before for $75,000. The winner was an anonymous bidder on the phone.

But what does this mean for the art world and for AI research?
